"Pi", what is it?


Pi (π), usually expressed as the lowercase form of the sixteenth letter of the Greek alphabet, is the mathemtical constant which represents the ratio of the circumference of a circle over its diameter. This mathematical constant appears all throughout circles regardless of shape or size, always giving the same value of 3.14, when dividing the circumference of a circle over its diameter. Pi is known to be one of the most famous mathematical constants, being introduced to third graders or primary 3 students.
